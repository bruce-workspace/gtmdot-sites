# Impeccable design context — gtmdot-sites

This file is read by the `impeccable` skill before any design work. It captures
the long-running design context for the GTMDot prospect-site builder so design
sessions don't have to re-derive it every time.

## What this product is

GTMDot builds **prospect websites for Atlanta-area trade businesses** —
collision repair, electricians, painters, plumbers, HVAC, mobile mechanics,
landscape, drywall, etc. Family-run, owner-operated, ~5–25 employee shops with
a 4.5–5.0★ Google rating and real referral business.

Each site is a **multi-page scaffold** (homepage, services, about, contact, plus
one SEO page per service the business offers). It's generated from
`templates/multi-page/` filled with per-business data + Bruce's photos + real
verbatim Google/Yelp reviews. Mini deploys to Cloudflare Pages.

The prospect doesn't know we built it. We deliver it as outbound (postcard or
email) with a claim code — "we already built your site, you can take it for $49
the first month, $149 ongoing." The site has to feel **owned, finished, and
proudly the prospect's** the moment they land — not like a marketing demo.

## Target audience

Two very different audiences see every site, in this order:

1. **The prospect (the trade-business owner).** 35–65 years old, Atlanta metro,
   reads on a phone in a service truck or breakroom. They've seen Wix /
   GoDaddy / Yelp / Angi templates a thousand times and instinctively distrust
   anything that looks generic. They trust photos of *their* shop, *their*
   crew, and *real reviews from people they remember*. The site has to read as
   "someone built me a real site that takes my business seriously" — not "an
   AI made me a brochure."

2. **The prospect's customer (a homeowner).** Looking for a body shop /
   electrician / plumber / etc. They land via Google or postcard QR. They want
   three things in 15 seconds: (a) is this a real local business with real
   reviews, (b) can I see what the work looks like, (c) how do I get an
   estimate. The site exists to convert this person to a phone call or estimate
   form fill.

Both audiences need to feel **personality and craft**, not blandness.

## Use cases (jobs to be done)

For the **prospect on first view**:
- Recognize their own business. See their year founded, real review count,
  real photos, real services.
- Feel proud of it — it should look better than what they would've paid a
  freelance web designer $3K for.
- Be motivated to claim the site (single CTA: claim with the code on the
  postcard / email).

For the **homeowner customer**:
- Confirm legitimacy in <5 seconds (rating + review count + year founded).
- Browse what the business does without scrolling forever.
- Read 3–5 real reviews in their own words.
- Submit an estimate request (form at the bottom of every page, plus phone
  number always visible in nav and CTA bands).

## Brand personality / tone

Three concrete words for the brand voice — pick one of these clusters per
business but the SCAFFOLD must support all three:

- **"Hand-built, opinionated, family-feel"** — pine-peach-painting, jack-glass-electric,
  forest-park-collision. Editorial dark mode, serif display, vibrant accent
  per vertical, real owner quotes, story 2-col with owner photo + callout.
- **"Warm-amber, campaign-energy, friendly-pro"** — happy-hvac. Lighter ink,
  hero-quote sidebar, kicker badges, status dot ("answering calls now"),
  trust pills as a badge strip.
- **"Quiet, refined, no fluff"** — for the rare prospect whose own brand reads
  premium-minimalist. Less ornament, more whitespace, but still custom-feeling.

Default for unverified verticals: **editorial dark mode with the per-vertical
accent color**. That's the canonical reference (pine-peach + jack-glass).

## What the site is NOT

- ❌ Substack-blog flat. ❌ Wix/Squarespace generic. ❌ "AI slop" — gradient
  hero + Inter everywhere + pill button + "Built by AI" energy.
- ❌ Maximalist for its own sake. We're not an art-direction portfolio.
- ❌ Stocky. No Unsplash hero, no smiling-handshake photography, no
  "Verified Customer" fake reviews. Every photo and review must be real.
- ❌ Generic CTAs. No "Get Started" or "Learn More." Use the verb the
  business actually uses: "Get an Estimate," "Schedule Service," "Book a Tow."

## Required components (every site needs all of these)

Per Jesse's feedback 2026-04-27 ("there's always a form at the bottom" /
"I can see elements of the new design but it's missing so much"):

1. **Fixed nav with backdrop-blur** — logo (single mark, not duplicated),
   primary nav links, phone number, primary CTA button, hamburger on mobile.
2. **Hero** — kicker badge ("EST. {year} · {category}"), status dot ("Now
   serving {city}"), serif h1, body lede, primary CTA + phone ghost button,
   italic blockquote pulling from BUSINESS_TAGLINE. Background photo with
   inkwash overlay.
3. **Trust strip** — 4-up stat band overlapping the hero. YEAR_FOUNDED,
   GBP_RATING with ★, GBP_REVIEW_COUNT, "$0 / Free estimates."
4. **Services** — 4-up grid of dark cards with brand-glow icon tile + svc-foot
   arrow that animates on hover.
5. **Reviews** — PATH A (3+) horizontal scroll cards, PATH B (1–2) centered
   single card + GBP link, PATH C (0) empty-state card with GBP link.
6. **Work gallery** — 6 ambiguous-photo-slot tiles with hover scale, captions
   that fade in.
7. **Story / about callout** — owner section with story-callout component
   (large opening smart quote, italic, accent color).
8. **Service area pills** — wraps cleanly, hover state to accent.
9. **FAQ accordion** — native `<details>` with `+` → `×` rotation.
10. **Estimate form section** — present on EVERY page (not just contact.html),
    near the bottom. Dark surface inputs, accent focus glow, drag/drop upload
    area. (Jesse called this out specifically as missing.)
11. **CTA band** — final section before footer. Eyebrow, h2, primary CTA
    button, phone tail link.
12. **Footer** — dark sunk surface, accent-light h4 labels in caps with
    0.18em tracking.

## Constraints

- **Single template token in CSS:** `--accent: {{VERTICAL_ACCENT_COLOR}}`. All
  shades derived via `color-mix()`. One hex swap restyles the whole site.
- **Type:** Cormorant Garamond (display) + Plus Jakarta Sans (body) + ui-monospace
  (label). Loaded from Google Fonts. Defined as `--font-serif` / `--font-sans`
  / `--font-mono`.
- **Component class names are LOAD-BEARING.** `.site-header`, `.wrap`,
  `.cta-btn`, `.hero`, `.service-grid`, `.service-card`, `.reviews-bar`,
  `.review-mini`, `.gtmdot-photo-slot`, `.contact-form`, `.site-footer` etc.
  are inspected by `pre-push-gate.sh`, `verify-build.sh`,
  `consume-asset-intel.py`, `render-reviews-bar.py`, and Mini's
  `_shared/claim-ui.html` injector. Don't rename them.
- **No claim bar / popup / cookie banner in R1VS-built HTML.** Mini injects
  those at deploy time from the shared template. Anything R1VS ships with a
  `#claimBar`, `#exitPopup`, or `#cookieBanner` selector will get
  `display: none !important`-ed by the injector.
- **Photos are placeholder paths only.** R1VS uses `photos/hero.jpg`,
  `photos/gbp-1.jpg` etc. Bruce delivers real photos in `photos-raw/` +
  `photos-generated/`. Mini integrates at deploy. The `.gtmdot-photo-slot`
  pattern handles the placeholder/integration distinction.
- **Reviews must be verbatim.** No "Verified Customer," no "Google Customer,"
  no fabricated testimonial copy. `pre-push-gate.sh` greps for known
  hallucination strings.
- **Accessibility floor:** WCAG AA contrast on all text/background pairs,
  keyboard-navigable nav + form, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected.

## Differentiation — the one thing that should be unforgettable

The **kicker + serif h1 + status dot combo** in the hero plus the
**4-up trust strip** that overlaps the hero. That signature stack —
"EST. 2008 · COLLISION REPAIR" stamped in the accent color, then a
story-feel serif headline, then a green pulse "NOW SERVING FOREST PARK,"
then a 4-pillar stat band that floats over the bottom edge of the hero
— is what makes a GTMDot site read as "someone built me a real site"
in the first 3 seconds.

Everything else (cards, gallery, form, footer) supports that opening move.

## Voice / copy

- Use the prospect's own words wherever possible. Pull headlines from
  RESEARCH.md "voice notes." Pull review quotes verbatim. Pull tagline from
  BUSINESS_TAGLINE.
- Headlines are statements, not questions. "Forest Park collision repair,
  done so well you can't tell it happened" beats "Need collision repair?"
- Sentence case for body, Title Case for buttons and footer headings,
  UPPERCASE with 0.18em tracking for kicker / eyebrow / footer h4 labels.
- "Free estimate," "same-week scheduling," "insurance work welcome" — the
  trade-business-real promises. Not "AI-powered" / "industry-leading" /
  "cutting-edge."
