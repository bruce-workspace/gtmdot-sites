About this service

What gets installed

A Level 2 charger (240V, typically 40 or 48 amps) is the practical home standard — gives you about 25–30 miles of range per hour of charging. Everyone we install is code-compliant, GFCI-protected at the breaker, and grounded properly.

We install whatever you buy: Tesla Wall Connector, ChargePoint Home Flex, Wallbox Pulsar Plus, Grizzl-E, Emporia. If you don't have one yet, we'll tell you which makes sense for your car and panel capacity.

Panel load check comes first

Before anything gets wired, we do a panel load calculation per NEC Article 220. The question is whether your existing panel can handle a 40 or 48-amp continuous draw on top of your current load. About 30% of Atlanta homes we look at need a subpanel or a panel upgrade first.

If your panel has room, installation is a half-day job: new 240V circuit, conduit run from panel to charger location (garage, driveway, side of house), hardware mount, and a quick test with your car. We pull the permit and schedule the inspection.

What to expect

  1. Free on-site load calc — we measure your existing panel capacity and tell you straight up if it'll handle the EV charger.
  2. If load is fine: quote the install. If load is marginal: we quote both the EV install AND the subpanel or main upgrade needed.
  3. Install day — typically half a day of work, starting with panel-off time.
  4. Permit inspection + a test charge with your car. You plug in and we confirm it's delivering full amperage.

FAQ

Common questions about EV Charger Installation.

How much does EV charger installation cost?

A straightforward Level 2 install with a short conduit run is typically $700–$1,200 including permit. Longer runs, trenching, or panel work push it higher. We quote after we see the site.

Will I need a panel upgrade?

About 30% of Atlanta homes we assess do. If your panel is 200-amp and not already loaded up, you're probably fine. If it's 100-amp or you've already added a lot of circuits (finished basement, heat pump, etc.), you may need a subpanel or main upgrade.

Do you handle the permit?

Yes. We pull the electrical permit and schedule the inspection. You get the signed permit card — helps with home resale and some EV rebate programs.
