About this service

When you need a panel upgrade

Most Atlanta homes built before 2005 have a 100-amp or 150-amp main panel. That was plenty when the only big loads were a stove, an HVAC unit, and a water heater. Add an EV charger, a heat pump water heater, or a finished basement with a mini-split, and you run out of service capacity fast.

Signs you need an upgrade: breakers tripping when multiple large appliances run; no room in the panel for new circuits; aluminum bus bar with corrosion; Federal Pacific or Zinsco panel (both are safety recalls and a frequent insurance disqualifier in Georgia).

What the upgrade involves

The upgrade itself is a day's work on most homes. We coordinate with Georgia Power for the temporary disconnect, install a new 200-amp main panel with modern Square D or Siemens hardware, transfer all circuits, and have the inspection scheduled the same week. You're without power for about 4 hours on the cutover day.

Permit and inspection fees are itemized separately so you see the true breakdown. We pull the permit, we schedule the inspection, we deal with any corrections. You get a signed-off permit card at the end.

What to expect

  1. Free on-site assessment — we look at the existing panel, the service drop, and your future load plans.
  2. Written quote including parts, labor, permit, and Georgia Power coordination.
  3. Scheduled install day — typically 1 day of work with ~4 hours of power-off.
  4. Inspection coordinated through the city; you get the signed permit card for your records.

FAQ

Common questions about Panel Upgrades.

How much does a 200-amp panel upgrade cost in Atlanta?

Most residential 200-amp upgrades fall in the $2,500–$4,500 range including permit fees and Georgia Power coordination. Price varies based on the length of the service drop, how much relocation is needed, and whether the grounding/bonding system needs updating. We quote after the on-site assessment so there are no surprises.

How long does it take?

Most upgrades are one day of work. Your power is off for about 4 hours during the cutover; the rest of the time we're working inside the panel with the main breaker off.

Do you pull the permit?

Yes. We pull the electrical permit with the City of Atlanta (or whichever jurisdiction applies) and schedule the inspection. You get the signed-off permit card at completion — useful for home resale and insurance.
