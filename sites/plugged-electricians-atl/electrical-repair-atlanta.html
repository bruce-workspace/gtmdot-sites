What we fix

Most electrical problems in older Atlanta homes trace back to one of four things: a failing breaker, a loose connection behind a switch or outlet, overloaded circuits, or worn wiring in a wet area (kitchen, bathroom, outdoor). We start every repair call with a load check and a visual on the panel — that usually narrows the cause in under 15 minutes.

We quote the repair before we start. If what you need is a breaker replacement, that's one number. If the diagnosis reveals something bigger — say, aluminum wiring in a 1970s home, or a panel that's been flagged by an inspection — we'll tell you what the full scope looks like and let you decide whether to fix it now or phase it.

How diagnosis works

Flickering lights are the most common call. About 60% of the time the cause is a loose neutral on the circuit, which is a quick fix. The other 40% splits between an overloaded circuit (especially in old homes where one 15-amp circuit is feeding half a floor), a failing light fixture, or — in the worst case — a utility-side voltage fluctuation that needs Georgia Power to look at.

Dead outlets are usually a tripped GFCI somewhere else on the same circuit, a wire that's come loose behind the outlet, or a failed outlet that needs replacement. We carry common-replacement outlets on the truck so we can usually fix it on the first visit.

Most straightforward repairs (outlet replacement, breaker swap, switch fix) are in the $150–$300 range including parts. Bigger repairs involving rewiring or panel work are quoted after diagnosis — always before we start.

Are you licensed and insured in Georgia?

Yes. We're a licensed electrical contractor in the state of Georgia and carry general liability plus workers' comp insurance. Happy to send copies before we come out.

Can you handle emergency calls?

We answer the phone 24/7 for emergencies. A true emergency (sparks, smoke, smell of burning, complete power loss) gets a same-night visit. Non-emergencies get scheduled for next available slot.
